Temples to be closed today for lunar eclipse

Major temples, including the Sri Meenakshi Sundareswarar Temple in Madurai and the Sri Dhandayuthapaniswamy Temple in Palani, Dindigul, will remain closed for several hours on Tuesday for the total lunar eclipse.
Madurai: Major temples, including the Sri Meenakshi Sundareswarar Temple in Madurai and the Sri Dhandayuthapaniswamy Temple in Palani, Dindigul, will remain closed for several hours on Tuesday for the total lunar eclipse.
A statement from A Arunachalam, deputy commissioner/ executive officer, Meenakshi temple, said the temple will be closed for darshan for 10 hours from 9.30am to 7.30pm in view of the eclipse.
Devotees will be allowed for darshan only after 7.30pm. The âAnnabhishekamâ will be performed at 7am. It was also stated that similar arrangements apply to all 22 satellite temples of the Meenakshi temple.
Meanwhile, joint commissioner of the Palani templs N Natarajan, in a statement, said the entry of devotees through steps, winch and rope car will be stopped by 11.30am and tickets also will not be sold. The temple will be closed from 12pm to 7pm. Special pujas would be performed after which by 7pm the devotees would be allowed again for darshan.
